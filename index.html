    Publications

    All publications on Google Scholar

    1. Comprehensive Exploration of Synthetic Data Generation: A Survey


      André Bauer, Simon Trapp, Michael Stenger, Robert Leppich, Samuel Kounev, Mark Leznik , Kyle Chard, Ian Foster


      A huge journal publication diving into various approaches of synthetic data generation. This work surveys 417 Synthetic Data Generation models over the last decade, providing a comprehensive overview of model types, functionality, and improvements.


    3. [SoK] The Great GAN Bake Off, An Extensive Systematic Evaluation of Generative Adversarial Network Architectures for Time Series Synthesis


      Mark Leznik, Arne Lochner, Stefan Wesner, Jörg Domaschka


      This work provides a methodologial evaluation of several GAN architectures (LSTM, CNN, Transformer) and showcases a pipeline of objectively evaluating the results of GANs, as well as recognizing "mode collapse"


    5. Same, Same, but Dissimilar: Exploring Measurements for Workload Time-series Similarity


      Mark Leznik, Johannes Grohmann, Nina Kliche, André Bauer, Daniel Seybold, Simon Eismann, Samuel Kounev, Jörg Domaschka


      In this work, we look at numerous measures for comparing time series measurements runs. This allows for a more precise assesment of the underlying data, rather than relying on simple mean and std. deviation evaluation.


    7. Multivariate Time Series Synthesis Using Generative Adversarial Networks


      Mark Leznik, Patrick Michalsky, Peter Willis, Benjamin Schanzel, Jörg Domaschka


      This is the background paper that provided the foundation for my thesis, we have shown time series synthesis to be a feasible using GANs and LSTMs with more than 500 timesteps. This work is currently also my most cited paper, as it provides a baseline of time series synthesis using GANs.
